Story of Brave Islanders

A long time ago, on a quiet island in the Pacific Ocean, many people lived happily. They loved their home very much — the tall green trees, the blue ocean with colorful fish, and the special songs and dances they shared.

One day, some builders came and wanted to cut down the trees and build big buildings. The island people didn’t want to lose their home, so they came together and said, “No, please don’t hurt our island!” They stood quietly and bravely, holding hands and singing their songs to show how much they cared.

Because of their courage, the trees stayed tall, the ocean stayed clean, and the island stayed beautiful. Now, children and their children can enjoy this special place, just like before.

Learn About Our Traditions

The lei is a traditional flower necklace that holds deep meaning in many Pacific Island cultures. It represents love, respect, and community. In the Taro Catch Game, the lei acts as a protective shield power-up, symbolizing the strength and support found in Pacific Island traditions. Wearing a lei shows honor and connection to others, which helps players feel resilient against challenges like pollution and harm in the game. The lei is important because it reminds us of the cultural values that unite Pacific Island communities. It encourages respect for nature and each other, which is essential for protecting their land, heritage, and way of life.
